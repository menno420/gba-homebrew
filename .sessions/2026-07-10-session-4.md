## Guard recipe (for the next session)

Scripted-input planning against the emulator: an exact bn::fixed physics
replica (12-bit half-precision multiplies, collide-and-slide port) matches
mGBA for SOLID key holds but diverges under 1-frame key pulses — the
game-loop keypad poll jitters ±1 frame vs harness frames, so hand-tuned
pulse lists are NOT reproducible from a sim. What works: (1) route-plan in
the sim, (2) use `--keys-pattern` duty cycles (shift-invariant under
constant latency) instead of pulse lists, (3) make every phase begin and
end on a SETTLE (ledge rest / wall pin / corner) — settles zero the
velocity and snap position, absorbing all accumulated timing error, then
(4) tune each phase's boundary empirically in the emulator (bisect on the
outcome, e.g. burst length 645→650 flipped death-at-24 to death-at-34).
Anchors: the key spans in `.github/workflows/headless-boot.yml`
`lumen-drift-proof` job. Also: the surge kills ceiling camps (~frame 160
at the cave mouth) — no resync point above the surge line is usable.
