## Guard recipe (for the next slice — barricades)

- **Night-1 bit-equivalence is the cheap-repin trick:** slice 4 kept all
  36 slice-3 pins by making night 1 identical (wave of 1 at frame 0, no
  A pressed in old proofs). Barricades can do the same: an untouched
  yard must step identically — keep new state inert until the new verb
  is used, and the old pins survive.
- **gloam-route.py autopilot knows nothing about barricades:** its
  rollout in `rollout_score()` must mirror any main.c step-order change
  (spawn → player → shove → zombie steps → contact) or derived routes go
  stale — the tool asserts nothing by itself; the emulator replay is the
  ground truth that catches drift.
- **GL_STRESS discipline:** always `make clean` between shipped and
  stress builds (the Makefile comment says so; CI builds stress FIRST
  then cleans) — a stale object mix is the one way to ship a no-death
  ROM. The shipped-vs-stress tell in telemetry: night-1 `t[17]`
  (ZTOTAL) is 1 shipped, 24 stress.
- **Frame-cost probe caveat:** GL_T_VLMAX is since power-on, so the
  night-start console-redraw spike (133 lines) dominates it forever;
  assert steady state on GL_T_VLINES at a quiet frame, budget the spike
  only against 263. If barricades add draw calls, re-measure BOTH.
- The `keypad_update(0)` boot trap + ROM `pad_seen_idle` guard + the
  ±2-frame emulator/ROM alignment (with occasional single-frame skips)
  all still apply — routes need ≥10 px margin, movement-skew-verified.
