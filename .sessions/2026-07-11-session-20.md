## 💡 Session idea

The duel-WIN proof forces this lane's first CLOSED-LOOP route recorder:
Gloamline's dawn route was derived by a scratch search over open-loop
inputs, but out-dueling a firing AI needs a policy (steer to hold the
enemy abeam, fire on alignment) run against the host mirror, with the
recorded input trace replayed open-loop in the emulator. If the
policy-recorded route survives ±-frame alignment shifts like the dawn
route did, the recorder pattern generalizes and Gloamline's slice-4+
waves can adopt it; if it proves brittle, that brittleness is the
finding — record it in the guard recipe either way.

  pinned assert values: rerun `record-duel-win.py` (CI diffs the
  committed route against a fresh recording, so staleness fails fast),
  re-pin proof numbers from a local headless run, and re-run the seed
  scan if the duel dynamics changed — expect most seeds to be
  chaos-fragile; demand the full ±6 window like this slice did.
- The frame-mapping law (T-A completed steps; seed = A-2) held exact
  across all four proofs — trust it, but always assert the latched
  seed FIRST so a mapping break fails loudly at the top of a proof.
- Colors: proof PNGs are R/B-swapped (tool quirk, PLATFORM-LIMITS) —
  judge palettes on a real emulator, never from CI artifacts.
- Affine sprites: hide = set as regular sprite that frame (no hide bit
  in rotate/scale mode); pattern in `draw_ships_and_balls()`.
