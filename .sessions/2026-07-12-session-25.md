## 💡 Session idea

The salvage phase is the arc's first live-water state that is NOT a
duel: the enemy is gone, so the world is pure player kinematics — which
makes recorded routes over it dramatically more robust than duel routes
(no closed-loop adversary reshaping the run per frame). The measured
flip side: a salvage route recorded off a duel's END STATE inherits the
duel's chaos — seed 126's win is shift-robust as an outcome but its end
state teleports under a -5..-3 frame skew (a different duel, won ~90
steps earlier, wreck ~25 px away). Rule extracted for future recorders:
**a route that CONTINUES past a chaotic phase needs its anchor seed
scanned for end-state stability, not just outcome stability.** Seed 127
passed the full [-6,+6] window for both; the scan is cheap (mirror-only,
~284 seeds in seconds).
