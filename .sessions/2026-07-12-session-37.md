## 💡 Session idea

Design the inverse as "mid-span of the forward map's preimage" and the
round-trip becomes an EXACT provable property instead of a ±1-cell
approximation. The naive touch inverse (linear pixel-to-yard scaling)
failed the cell round-trip at integer-division boundaries — tap row 6,
mark renders in row 5. The fix wasn't tolerance ("assert within ±1")
but construction: for forward map f(py) = (py-32)*14/144, place the
mark at the MIDPOINT of f's preimage interval for the tapped cell —
y = 32 + (k*144 + 72)/14 — and f(y) == k holds for every cell, provable
by exhaustion host-side (392 cells, 49,152 touch pixels) with zero
emulator involvement. Transferable rule for the lane: when a feature
needs an inverse of an integer-truncating render map, don't invert the
formula — pick the canonical point inside the forward map's preimage;
exactness then survives any future retuning of the map's dimensions,
and the proof is a three-line loop instead of a tolerance argument.
