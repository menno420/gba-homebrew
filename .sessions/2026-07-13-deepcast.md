Bite-hooking is automatic/forgiving by design — the feel is patience/push-pull, NOT reflex taps (no overlap with Undertow). ## 💡 Session idea

Deepcast's proof was authored in five short emulator runs by a **listen-then-script loop**: run the ROM with `--watch-log` and no plan, read the seeded timeline out of the CSV (when the fish rests, when it surges, when the bite lands), then write input spans that play WELL against that exact timeline — and only then pin asserts at the frames the log already showed. For any deterministic game this turns proof-writing from "guess and re-tune constants" into transcription, and it produces evidence with narrative value: the committed script demonstrably plays the intended strategy (reel the rests, yield the surges), not just any input soup that reaches the end state. A generic `tools/` helper that diffs two watch-logs and proposes pin frames (state transitions + extrema) would make this a ten-minute job for every future title.
