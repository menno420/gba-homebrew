## 💡 Session idea

**A "hot path in IWRAM" claim is a MAP-FILE claim, not a build-rule
claim — and a profiler probe is itself code that can lie to you.**
Two traps from one session, both caught by refusing to trust a
number that lacked a cross-check: (1) Butano's `*.bn_iwram.cpp` rule
compiles ARM with long calls but does NOT place the section — the
"IWRAM" flock pass sat at 0x08xxxxxx (ROM) as 32-bit ARM on a 16-bit
bus, the WORST placement, and the measurement barely moved; only
`__attribute__((section(".iwram")))` (Butano's own BN_CODE_IWRAM
value) put it at 0x03000000, and the mean fell 93.4% → 52.4% in one
step. Rule: **any placement optimization is verified by address in
the .map, never by filename or flag.** (2) My first timer probe read
"the flock costs 6.2%" because the probe wrote its ticks into a
telemetry word that the normal frame code overwrote two lines later
— run_frames masquerading as a measurement. Rule: **a probe's output
channel must be a word nothing else writes** — and when two
instruments disagree (Butano's meter said 93%, my probe said 6%),
the disagreement IS the finding; bisect until they agree before
optimizing anything. The profiling ladder that emerged (naive 120.5%
→ grid 103.3% → cap 96.9% → real IWRAM 52.4%) also carries a design
lesson: **cohesion defeats spatial grids exactly when the flock
flocks** — a boids neighbor grid degenerates to O(n^2) at the
moment the mechanic works, so the deterministic per-fish neighbor
cap, not the grid, is what bounds the work.
