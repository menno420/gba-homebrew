## 💡 Session idea

**An audit's most load-bearing lines are its nulls — but only if every
"not found" names its search.** The single practice that most changed
this audit's trustworthiness was recording the exact probe next to
each negative claim (`git log -S "Auto-Mode Bypass"` = 0 hits, grep
for a staleness quirk = absent, inbox tops out at ORDER 005). A "not
found" with its search command is falsifiable and cheap to re-run;
a bare "didn't happen" is just vibes. Corollary discovered while
enforcing the public/private split: naive isolation greps lie in both
directions — my `patch` pattern false-positived on "dis**patch**" and
"PATCH …/git/refs" dozens of times, so a leak-scan needs word
boundaries plus a human pass, and the *absence* of hits should itself
be recorded with the pattern used.
