## 💡 Session idea

**For a turn-based game, write the sim twice — once in the ROM and
once as a 100-line host mirror — and make the mirror the route
planner, not just a checker.** Every prior lane derived its committed
routes by probing the ROM (build, script, watch-log, adjust, repeat —
Shoal needed seven instrumented builds before its first pin). Because
Wickroad's whole sim is a closed form of (day, impact), the mirror
planned the entire 13-day winning route BEFORE the first C++ line
compiled, and the first ROM probe matched it leg for leg — zero
route iterations on emulated hardware, and every transcribed pin got
a free independent cross-check (two implementations agreeing beats
one implementation observed). The enabling design choice, worth
repeating on any future turn-based concept: keep the state
transition a pure function of (inputs so far), never of frames — then
the input script IS the run, the mirror is trivially exact, and
"run-twice byte-identity" stops being a property you test and becomes
one you constructed. Corollary: the mailbox should carry the hook,
not just the state — Wickroad's words 14/15 exist so "the ledger
lies" is one watch assert instead of an argument in prose.
